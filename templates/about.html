About Current System

In the recent years, plant physiologists, biotechnologists, and eco-physiologists have been working to investigate various other blind features of the primary and secondary macronutrients and their future prospective, because macronutrients are involved in every step of plant life. As every macronutrient has its own character, therefore they are involved in different metabolic processes of plant life. The pH is measured by means of 1:2.5 H2O method, the organic carbon is measured in percentage by Walkley-Black method and the electrical conductivity measured in Deci Siemens per metre (dS/m). All of the macronutrients (N, P, K, S) are measured in Kg/Ha while the remaining micronutrients (Zn, Fe, Cu, Mn, B) are measured in parts per million (ppm). Determining the level of each macronutrient in soil is necessary to maintain and enhance its productivity, avoid soil degradation problems, improve fertilizers and overcome environmental damages. Thus, the necessity of developing an effective nutrient management arises by means of an adequate soil analysis and a proper application of fertilizers. Classification of soil parameters such as the fertility indices for macronutrients (Nitrogen, Potassium, Phosphorus), soil pH, soil type, preferred crop is relevant for fertilizer recommendation.

This system aims to classify the fertility levels of primary macro-nutrients of soil i.e., Nitrogen, Potassium and Phosphorus based on the soil test report values. The classification and prediction of the fertility levels of these macro-nutrients aids in recommending a suitable amount of fertilizer, thus saving the time of specialized technicians in developing expensive chemical analysis.
